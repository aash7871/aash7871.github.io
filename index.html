About Me

I'm a 5th year grauate student at the Ohio State University. I study stars with masses near to and less massive than our sun. My work has applied a range of techniques including theoretical modelling and asteroseismology to disentangle stellar physics. I am most interested in questions pretaining to stellar rotation and magnetism and have worked on rotational evolution models and stellar winds.

Both in my work and outside of it I enjoy excersicing creativity. I have strong interest in scientific illustration and communication.

Research

For an in-depth look at my research, visit my ADS library.

Luminous Giant Asteroseismology

Asteroseismology, a techniqe which allows a stellar oscillations to be related to fundamental stellar properties, has revolutionized our ability to measure large stellar populations. At the core of asteroseismology is the scaling relations. In this work I investigate the origins of a known breakdown of these relations in the most luminous giant stars.

In my current research I test theoretical stellar evolution models against observational constraints acrosss the HR-diagram. My main interests are in improving our understanding of and ability to understand stellar processes. However, in my work I also strive to improve inferences of stellar properties such as age and mass so that we can better understand other astrophysical processes such as planet formation and habitability.

Rotational Modeling of Old Partially-Convective Stars

In this work, I expanded our understanding of rotational evolution and gyrochronology to the old field star domain. Historically, open clusters have served as the laboratory for constraining rotational evolution of solar-like stars. However, by using kinematic ages, I have modeled the rotation of old, slowly rotating partially-convective stars and have revealed potential missing physics in our rotation models.

Towards the Fully-Convective Domain

Followig my work in the partially-convective domain, I extend my analysis to the lower mass fully-convective domain. The rotational evolution od these stars is of particular interest as these stars have a different structure than solar-like stars. As rotational evolution is driven by magnetism, studying the spin down of these stars may illuminate how these stars generate magnetic fields and whether this mechnaism is similar to partially-convective stars. In this domain, I especially focus on understanding the properties of the stellar wind.
